Frequently Asked Questions

What is NITS?

NITS (Neuro-Oncological Integrated Treatment System) is an advanced medical device that combines multiple imaging technologies (X-ray, CT, MRI, Angiogram) into one unified system for faster, more precise cancer diagnosis and treatment.

How fast is NITS treatment?

Most patients complete their treatment by noon, compared to a full day (8+ hours) with traditional methods. This includes diagnosis, imaging, treatment planning, and the procedure itself.

Is NITS safe?

Yes. NITS actually reduces radiation exposure compared to traditional methods that require multiple separate imaging devices. The integrated system is designed with patient safety as a priority and meets all international medical standards.

Will NITS hurt?

NITS procedures are minimally invasive and designed for patient comfort. Most patients experience minimal discomfort, and many report it's less stressful than traditional multi-device approaches due to shorter treatment time.

How is NITS different from traditional cancer treatment?

Traditional cancer treatment often requires multiple appointments, multiple devices, and extended hospital stays. NITS consolidates everything into one efficient session with superior imaging precision and significantly reduced treatment time.

Can my hospital get NITS?

Yes! NITS is available for hospital integration. We work with medical facilities to implement the system and train staff. Contact us for facility consultation and partnership opportunities.

What cancers can NITS treat?

NITS is particularly effective for neuro-oncological cases (brain and spinal tumors) but can be adapted for various cancer types. Your medical team will determine if NITS is appropriate for your specific condition.

How do I schedule a NITS consultation?

Contact us through our website or call our patient coordinator. We'll connect you with a medical professional who can evaluate your condition and discuss whether NITS is the right option for you.
